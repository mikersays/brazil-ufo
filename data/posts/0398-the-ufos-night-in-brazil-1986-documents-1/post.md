“Report. Day 19 to May 20, 1986.

Time: start 23: 15Z – End: 03: 40Z

At 11:15 pm, OI BR, 3S Daniel received a TF1 call (sector F8) from the ACC-BS [Brasilia-DF Approach Control] informing that the TWR-SJ operator [São José dos Campos Control Tower- SP], was seeing lights (luminous points) near the Aerodrome. We contacted TWR-SJ (TF2 9-276 – CONS – A) and it was confirmed to us by the operator, so we made the predicted questionnaire, which is attached. We contacted APP-São Paulo and the operator informed us that there were some plots with radar support near SJ. When asked about the TWR-SJ operator, we could sometimes observe plots in the NE sector of the Aerodrome, which was verified by the TWR-SJ operator. At 00: 01Z, we contacted the LP.23 radar from São Roque [in São Paulo-SP], but this was not plotting anything (TF3 – 9/305 – OI-BR). TWR-SJ operator report:

Sergeant BCT Sérgio Mota, operator of TWR-SJ reported that at 21:30Z [18:30hs] he noticed a spotlight on the city in the Northwest sector of the Aerodrome and two spots close to the external marker. These foci had no defined shape or dimensions, they appeared to be the size of the “head of a matchstick”. The dominant colors were red and orange, but changed to green and yellow. The observation was made with the naked eye and with binoculars. The sky was clear, N / NE there was a layer of fog, low and 2/8 of cirrus.
