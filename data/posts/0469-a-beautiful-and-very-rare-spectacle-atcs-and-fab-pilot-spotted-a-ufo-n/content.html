DEC 16th, 1977. At 20:00, near the region of Vitória, State of Espírito Santo, the FAB C-95 2162 plane reported an unidentified flying object sighted about 20 NM and 20,000 ft from Vitória -ES.

Translation of “Transcription of occurrence in Mission Order Report – Device not identified”.

“Salvador, December 23, 1977.

From : Commander of the Air Base of Salvador-BAHIA.

To: Mr. Commander of COMCOS.

Subject: Transcription of Occurrence in Mission Order Report

I – This Command transcribes the Occurrence of Mission Order n. 065, of 14 Dec 77, aircraft C-95 2162, / ADM Mission, bound for Rio de Janeiro / Porto Alegre, for knowledge and measures deemed necessary by Your Excellency:

OPERATIONS

At approximately 20:00 on December 16th, 1977, when returning to Salvador, on the occasion of establishing contact / radio with Vitória for position message “Itapemirim” (entrance / terminal VT), we were informed by the controller in Vitória that there was a unidentified flying object, approximately 20 miles from the vertical VT and 20,000 feet of altitude (information given perhaps by a BOEING plane).

The strange device, according to the information from the controller, who was observing it with binoculars with seven more people, had characteristics similar to a “Mother Ship” and was surrounded by smaller ones that were around it and curved 180 degrees, while the largest ship remained almost stationary, with varying brightness and size.

The larger ship, according to the informant, sometimes had a slight tail and was a beautiful and very rare spectacle. He also informed that he called Brasília Center to try to establish radar contact.

As for the crew of our plane, it was only possible to see a huge ball, the color of the stars, but much larger, which increased and decreased in size and brightness. ANV’s visibility was limited due to sparse clouds that did not allow regular observation of the phenomenon.

Upon leaving the terminal, we were informed that the object had begun to move slowly towards the vertical of Vitória. Ass: / CAP AV – N. – CMT DA ANV. “
