# A pilot reports 3 UFO sightings during flights between Brasília-DF and Curitiba-PR [1993]

*2020-07-04T21:16:58-03:00*

JUNE 24th, 1993.

A commercial pilot on the aircraft prefix PT LSF reported three sightings, on three different days, from UFO during three flights from Brasília-DF to Curitiba-PR.

According to the pilot, the UFO varied in colors red, green, white and blue. Such lights and its high speed were the only characteristics that the pilot was able to describe from the unidentified flying object.

In the first sighting, the UFO remained above the aircraft of the reporting pilot, in the Northwest/West position, on the horizon, then below and then disappearing. Duration: 30 minutes. People on board: 4. Visibility conditions: top FL 370 [37,000 feet].

In the second sighting, according to the pilot, the same thing happened. Duration: 30 minutes. People on board: 2. Visibility conditions: clear sky.

In the third sighting, the UFO positioned itself to the Northeast then to the North and Northeast again. Duration: the entire flight on the Brasília/Curitiba route. People on board: 2. Visibility conditions: clear sky.

Regarding the UFO’s speed, the object followed the displacement of the Pilot’s plane = 320 kt [576 km / h].

According to the complementary data of the report made by the pilot for COMDABRA [Brazilian Aerospace Defense Command]:

“Bright lights. The beginning of the observation […] after takeoff from SBBR [Brasília Airport] and when passing at Abm AN.

First observation: 70 NM Sul SBGO [Airport of Goiás], ACC-BS [Air Traffic Controllers of Brasília Area Control Center] reported having RADAR detection speed 130 kt [240 km / h] to supersonic;

Second observation: the same position as the first; went to W and disappeared;

Third observation: right after SBBR takeoff. Followed the entire displacement of the aircraft on the SBBR / SBCT Route. Position 02 hours to 12 hours (lowered FL from aircraft 350). Close to Curitiba, it headed towards Ponta Grossa, Campo Largo and disappeared.

* Pilot called from Sis CT (to Afonso Pena [Curitiba Airport]).”
